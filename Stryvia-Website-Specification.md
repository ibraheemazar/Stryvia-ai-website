# Stryvia — Website build specification

A complete blueprint for strategy, design, content, and engineering.

---

## 1. What Stryvia is

Stryvia gives you the capability to build, create, and solve problems at the speed and scale that only large teams could reach before. You bring the problem, you direct the work, and the intelligence does the heavy lifting, so you walk away with a solution that is customized to you and entirely yours. You stay in control. You own the outcome. You move without waiting on anyone, in the easiest way possible.

This is amplification, not replacement and not delegation. The site sells what becomes possible for the visitor, never the removal of the people they used to depend on. The underlying truth is independence, speed, and effortlessness. The surface expression is capability, control, and ownership.

Stryvia plays three roles at once, and a visitor should feel all three without being handed a list of them. It consults, understanding the problem the way a senior advisor would before proposing anything. It enables, giving the visitor capability they did not have and could not easily assemble. And it executes, doing the heavy lifting and automating the work so the visitor is not left managing it. Consultant, enabler, and builder, with the visitor directing throughout.

The one line a visitor should repeat a week later, unprompted: **Stryvia is the intelligence you build with.**

Two rules govern the entire site. Stryvia is an intelligence that amplifies the visitor, never a firm you hire and never a tool that makes you do the work yourself; the visitor is always the author and the owner. And no other company, and no individual person, is centered or named anywhere; Stryvia speaks as an institution in its own right and stands alone.

## 2. The audiences

The site serves nine distinct buyers, each entering through their own door, each with a different fear and a different desire, all buying the same underlying capability. Every audience page and every piece of copy is written to one of these people, never to a generic everyone.

**The visionary, the person with the idea.** Has had ideas for years and has never been able to bring them to life, because every idea required assembling a full team of specialists they could not pull together. This is the most emotional audience. Their desire is to see the thing in their head become real, finally. Stryvia removes the wall between the idea and the existence of the idea.

**Entrepreneurs and business builders.** Building or growing a venture without the team and budget a venture used to demand. Their fear is being stuck while competitors move. Their desire is momentum and ownership. Stryvia is the way the venture moves now.

**COOs and operations leaders.** Need to run leaner and move faster without adding headcount and overhead for every new initiative. Their fear is cost and dependency on vendors and hires. Their desire is control and efficiency. Stryvia lets them execute initiatives that would otherwise require a new team.

**CEOs and business owners.** Carrying a business problem, often in an area they do not personally understand, facing the prospect of hiring consultants, strategists, and specialists to address it. Their desire is to solve the problem without assembling and managing a cast of experts. Stryvia understands the problem and helps them solve it.

**People entering a domain they do not understand.** The person who says "I would need a CFO, a strategist, a consultant, a designer" to do the thing in front of them. They are blocked by the dependency itself. Their desire is to act without first becoming an expert or hiring a row of them. Stryvia is the expertise on demand, with them in control.

**Creatives and producers.** Want to produce at a scale and quality that normally requires a full production apparatus. Their fear is being capped by resources and budget. Their desire is leverage inside their own craft. Stryvia lets them produce far beyond what their headcount should allow.

**Marketing and growth leaders.** Need campaigns, content, and growth without the cost and slowness of an agency or a large internal team. Their desire is output and speed they control. Stryvia is the growth capability they direct themselves.

**Enterprises and larger organizations.** Want serious capability without vendor lock-in, with their data kept in the region and handled to local standards. Their fear is dependency and exposure. Their desire is capability they own and control. Stryvia is infrastructure they build on rather than a vendor they are tied to.

**Strategic investors.** Want exposure to a defensible, regionally anchored AI company with a real moat. Their fear is a commodity wrapper. Their desire is a category-defining bet. The site makes the thesis self-evident.

## 3. The pillars

These are the differentiators the entire site rests on. Each one must come through on every page where it is relevant, and together they are the answer to "why Stryvia and not something else."

**Depth of understanding.** The intelligence understands a problem the way a senior team would, before it proposes anything. It asks, it pushes back on vagueness, it reads the visitor's level of expertise and meets it. This is why the solution fits the visitor rather than arriving as a generic template that needs rework.

**Orchestration.** The strongest and most defensible pillar. Stryvia makes many AI models and tools work together so the whole exceeds any single one of them. A single model might write you a script but cannot also turn it into a storyboard. Stryvia integrates the models so you get the script and the storyboard and everything between them, as one coherent piece of work. No single tool does this, and the orchestration logic is part of the moat.

**Ownership and control.** The visitor directs the work and owns what gets built. There is no lock-in and no dependency. The intelligence does the heavy lifting while authorship stays with the visitor.

**It knows its limits.** Stryvia recognizes when a problem exceeds what it can responsibly handle and says so, routing to human expertise rather than guessing. Stated openly, this is the trust pillar, and it makes everything else the site claims more believable.

**Effortlessness.** The easiest way possible, without the hassle of assembling and depending on people. The promise is not just capability but capability without friction.

**Speed.** Weeks where the old way took months, because there is no waiting on a team to be hired, briefed, and coordinated. Speed is both a practical benefit and the emotional relief of momentum.

**End to end.** Stryvia does not stop at advice. It understands the problem, builds the solution, and automates the execution. Consultant, builder, and operator in one, which is what separates it from a tool that only advises or only generates.

**Customized to you.** The output is shaped to the visitor's specific situation, not a generic deliverable that needs rework. The depth of understanding feeds directly into a solution that actually fits.

The defensibility for the technical reader and the investor follows from these: the moat is not the underlying AI models, which anyone can access, but the proprietary thinking model that approaches a problem in structured layers from understanding to solution to execution, the orchestration that makes multiple providers work as one, and the body of solved problems that deepens the reference base the intelligence reasons from over time. The models are horsepower. The thinking model and the orchestration are the moat.

The honesty posture over all of it: the site never says it is unfinished and never pretends it is finished. The tone is forward and present. It reveals no roadmap and no pipeline.

## 4. The conversion model

There is one primary conversion path and every section feeds it. A visitor experiences the live Chat, describes a real problem, and receives a genuine scoped response: what they are trying to do, how it would be approached, the shape and rough timeline of the work, and what they would own at the end. Beneath that response sits one action, "Let's build this together," which captures the visitor's intent and contact details and opens the path to a human who structures the engagement. The Chat proves the intelligence. The human closes the relationship.

A secondary capture exists for the visitor who is not ready to start a full conversation: the Early Access path in Section 6.20, which holds them, builds a sense of entry, and keeps them in reach. No section closes a sale on its own. Every section routes back to the conversation or to access.

## 5. Information architecture

The site is large and rich by design, deep rather than cluttered. Every section earns its place by answering a real question a real visitor is asking. A casual visitor can reach the live Chat in one move; a serious evaluator can go as deep as they want without getting lost. Chat is present on every page as the primary action, never a support bubble.

│

├── The intelligence  (the Brain: understand, orchestrate, execute, limits;
│                       interactive "how it thinks")

The top navigation carries How it works, Capabilities, Industries, For you, The intelligence, Pricing, and a single high-contrast Start button. The richer sections are reached through the footer, through the Capabilities, Industries, and How-it-works hubs, and through contextual links inside pages, so the top bar stays calm while the depth is one click away. For investors sits in the footer, not the top navigation. There is no roadmap page anywhere, and no page built around a founder.

## 6. The sections of the site, explained

Every section is explained in full: what it is, what goes in it, why it matters, how it converts, and how Chat lives inside it. A designer and a developer should be able to build each one from its explanation here.

### 6.1 The homepage, built as a directed sequence

The homepage is the most important surface on the site, and it is not a stack of blocks. It is directed, the way a film is directed, as a sequence of scenes where each scene has a single job and a single emotional beat, and the visitor is carried from recognizing themselves, to seeing proof, to feeling that something they thought was out of reach is now in front of them, to acting. The whole page should feel composed and intentional, with calm motion and pacing rather than decorative effect, because the feeling of being directed is itself part of the proof that there is one intelligence behind this.

**Scene one, the opening frame.** A single, confident line that names the visitor's quiet frustration and resolves it in the same breath, the frustration of having ideas and problems that always required a team they had to find, fund, and wait on. Beneath the line sits the live Chat, open and inviting, with a designed empty state rather than a blank box: a warm prompt asking what the visitor wants to build or solve, and beneath it a few tappable real problems drawn from the problem gallery, so the visitor who does not know where to begin has a way in with one tap. A restrained cinematic backdrop sets an intelligent, institutional tone. The emotional beat is recognition and possibility: this is for me, and I can start right now.

**Scene two, the proof in motion.** When the visitor types, the Chat does not merely reply, it visibly understands. It restates the problem in its own words, asks one sharp clarifying question if the problem needs it, and returns a structured scope: what you are trying to do, how it would be approached, the shape and rough timeline, and what you would own. The supporting intelligence behind the single voice can be glimpsed and inspected on demand, calmly, never as a chaotic transcript. The scene ends with the "Let's build this together" action. The emotional beat is belief: it actually understands me, and this is real. This interaction is the single most important thing on the entire site and receives the most engineering and prompt attention of anything built.

**Scene three, the turn.** A strong statement of the shift in what is now possible, which is where the conviction that a personal story might have carried now lives as a universal truth. For as long as building anything meant assembling people, ambition was capped by who you could hire and afford and coordinate. That cap is gone. You bring the problem, the intelligence builds, you direct and you own. The emotional beat is liberation and relief.

**Scene four, the wow.** The single most differentiated moment, where orchestration is made visible. A concrete demonstration that no single tool can do this: one model writes the script, but a single model cannot also produce the storyboard, and Stryvia integrates the models so the visitor gets the script, the storyboard, and everything between them as one coherent piece of work. Shown, not described. The emotional beat is astonishment: I have never seen AI do this.

**Scene five, the expanse.** Not a menu, a feeling that whatever the visitor brings, it can be built. Breadth presented as possibility across building products, launching ventures, producing content, running operations, modeling finance, and more, with the Solution Finder offered as the guided way in for anyone who feels the breadth as overwhelm. Links into Capabilities and Industries. The emotional beat is scope and ambition.

**Scene six, the gut-punch.** A sharp, visceral contrast of the old way and the Stryvia way: months, an assembled team, and a large budget, against weeks, the visitor directing, and a fraction of the cost, with an entry into the interactive estimator. The emotional beat is urgency: why would I ever do it the old way.

**Scene seven, the reassurance.** The fears answered in one calm band: you stay in control because you direct the work, you own what gets built with no lock-in, your data stays in the region, and the intelligence knows its limits and tells you when a problem exceeds what it can responsibly do. The emotional beat is trust and safety.

**Scene eight, the doors.** Elegant entry points for the different audiences, a quiet "which of these is you," each routing to its audience page or seeding the Chat with that person's framing. The emotional beat is belonging.

**Scene nine, the belief.** A short, powerful glimpse of the Manifesto, the worldview and the category claim, linking to the full statement. This is the soul of the page. The emotional beat is meaning: these people see the future I want to be part of.

**Scene ten, the return.** The page ends where it began, at the conversation, now with momentum, inviting the visitor to tell the intelligence what they want to build, with the Early Access path offered for the visitor who is not yet ready to start. The emotional beat is action.

The homepage never lists features, never shows pricing tiers, never presents a menu of finished products, never promises a delivery timeline, and never shows a roadmap.

### 6.2 How it works

Makes a non-technical visitor understand the method and feel in control of it. It explains the thinking model as a simple flow: the intelligence understands the problem, orchestrates the right models, builds the solution, and hands the visitor ownership. It then makes the director relationship explicit, the visitor sets the intent, makes the consequential decisions, and redirects the work when it drifts, while the intelligence does the heavy lifting. This is the section that disarms the fear of losing control, so the director framing is central and repeated. It closes by showing how a conversation becomes a structured engagement. Chat docked, seeded to invite the visitor to describe a problem and watch how it is approached. Targets searches around how AI can solve business problems and building with AI without a team.

### 6.3 Capabilities, and the twenty-one capability pages

The Capabilities overview presents the full range of what Stryvia can do as possibility, confidently and broadly, no domain treated as a lesser afterthought, each card stating an outcome the visitor wants rather than a feature, each leading to a full capability page.

Each individual capability page is one of the deepest parts of the site, one per domain, following the same pattern: an outcome-led headline; the problems this domain solves in real language; two to four sub-capabilities, the sub-services, each explaining what becomes possible and how the visitor stays in control; two to three scenarios specific to the domain written as present possibility; a short note on how the intelligence approaches this domain, including where orchestration matters; and a domain-framed Chat invitation. Sub-capabilities are always framed as capabilities, never finished software, never referencing any other company. The Chat on each page is seeded with that domain, and each page targets the long-tail problem phrases specific to it.

The twenty-one capability pages at launch: build a product or app; launch a new venture; brand and identity creation; creative and content production; advertising and campaigns; marketing and growth; web presence and SEO; content and copywriting at scale; operations and automation; process and workflow design; custom AI agents and assistants; CRM and customer systems; e-commerce setup and optimization; finance, modeling and forecasting; strategy and business intelligence; market and competitor research; data analysis and dashboards; sales enablement and pipelines; investor and pitch materials; document and contract drafting; and training and knowledge systems. Document and contract drafting carries an explicit handoff to human expertise where the stakes are high. The set expands once the thinking-model documentation defines the full domain map.

### 6.4 Industries

This is the third axis of the site, alongside the role-based audience pages and the function-based capability pages. Where capabilities answer what Stryvia can do and audiences answer who it is for, industries answer "do you understand my world." There is an Industries overview and a page per sector, each speaking the language, problems, and constraints of that industry, showing how the capabilities apply inside it, and carrying scenarios specific to it. The launch set is chosen for the regional market and for search demand: real estate and development, food and beverage and hospitality, retail and e-commerce, media and entertainment and production, healthcare, education, professional services, financial services, government and public sector, manufacturing and logistics, and technology and startups. Each industry page is a strong relevance surface for a buyer who wants to feel understood and a strong search surface for sector-specific queries. Chat on each page is seeded with the industry. This layer is also where the regional advantage, the understanding of how business works here and the commitment to keeping data in the region, lands most concretely for enterprise buyers.

### 6.5 Find your solution (the Solution Finder)

Twenty-one capabilities and eleven industries will overwhelm a visitor who does not already know what they need, and an overwhelmed visitor leaves. The Solution Finder is the guided way in. The visitor selects or describes their role and their problem, and the finder surfaces the most relevant capabilities, the most relevant industry context, and the scenarios closest to their situation, then opens the Chat already seeded with everything it gathered, so the first response is sharp and personal. It is part navigation aid and part qualification, and it captures the shape of the visitor's need even before the conversation begins. It is reached from the homepage expanse scene, from the Capabilities and Industries hubs, and from the main navigation.

### 6.6 Problem gallery

Lets a visitor recognize their own situation and step straight into the intelligence. A filterable wall of real problems people face, each stated in the visitor's own words: a product idea with no development team, a launch into three markets with one strategist, a need to produce broadcast-quality content without a production house, a business problem in a domain the owner does not understand. These are problems, not solutions, and make no claim of work already done. The conversion mechanism is the click: selecting a problem opens the Chat already seeded with it. Each problem doubles as a long-tail search surface.

### 6.7 Outcome showcase

The inverse of the problem gallery. Where the gallery shows the pain, the showcase shows what becomes possible: trajectories of capability gained, framed around what the visitor can now do, the speed they gain, the scale they reach, the ownership they keep, rather than around deliverables a vendor produced. Framed as capability gained, never as work done for a named client. Targets searches around what can be built with AI and the outcomes of AI capability.

### 6.8 Speed and cost (interactive estimator)

Makes the time and cost compression visceral, because speed and cost are among the strongest drivers for every audience. Alongside side-by-side narratives of the old way and the Stryvia way, an interactive estimator lets the visitor pick the kind of thing they want to build and see the rough contrast: the traditional path with its months, its assembled team, and its cost, against the Stryvia path with its weeks, the visitor directing, and a fraction of the cost. The interaction itself is engagement and light qualification, and it ends in a Chat invitation seeded with what the visitor chose. Framed as giving permission to move faster, never as undercutting anyone. Targets searches around the cost of building with AI and how long AI solutions take.

### 6.9 Works with everything (the integration layer)

Shows that Stryvia orchestrates the wider ecosystem and fits what the visitor already uses. At its center a clean diagram with Stryvia as the orchestrator, surrounded by the leading AI models, the visitor's existing tools, and their data sources as inputs and outputs, with a short narrative on how orchestration produces results no single tool can. The technical visitor reads the architecture and sees the moat; the non-technical visitor reads "this works with what I already have, no rip and replace." One of the two homes of the orchestration pillar. Targets searches around AI orchestration and AI that integrates multiple models and tools.

### 6.10 Compare (competitive transparency)

Helps a buyer choose by being honest about the alternatives: hiring a consulting firm, building an in-house team, using a no-code or single AI tool, each with its honest cost, time, and limitation, set against how Stryvia differs. Genuine help, not trash-talk, and the honesty itself builds trust while signaling that the company understands its own market. Targets searches around alternatives to consultants, AI versus agency, and the best way to build with AI.

### 6.11 See it in control (the director's dashboard preview)

Makes the director relationship visible and kills the fear that the intelligence builds without the visitor's say. A visual preview of the working interface, the choices the intelligence surfaced, the visitor making the decision, the work executing, presented as a guided walkthrough rather than a live, clickable product, clearly a preview and not a claim of a finished product. Conveys control as something the visitor can see and feel, not just read about.

### 6.12 For you (the audience pages)

One page per audience from Section 2, each a door framed in that buyer's own language and fears. The pattern is consistent: a headline naming their specific desire, their problems in their own words, how Stryvia changes their situation framed as amplification, two scenarios from their world, and a Chat invitation in their language. The same capability sits underneath all of them with a different framing on top, and every page is written around what the visitor gains, never around who they no longer need, which neutralizes the job-threat objection without ever raising it. Chat seeded to the audience; each page targets that audience's search terms.

### 6.13 The intelligence (the Brain)

Makes the moat legible to the technical buyer and investor, while the non-technical visitor can skip it. It explains how the intelligence understands a problem; how it orchestrates the right models, anchored by the script-and-storyboard example; how it executes and automates; and how it knows its limits. An interactive element lets the visitor watch, at a high level, how Stryvia approaches a sample problem in layers, from understanding to orchestration to solution, so the moat is experienced rather than asserted, without exposing anything proprietary. Plain language with supporting visuals, a clear high-level version and an expandable deeper version, every sentence held to the honest claims in Section 3, never overclaiming a proprietary model where the truth is proprietary orchestration and reasoning. Links onward to the investor thesis. Targets searches around how AI orchestration works and what makes an AI company defensible.

### 6.14 Examples gallery

Makes capability concrete through many scenarios across domains, audiences, and industries. A filterable gallery of scenario cards, each opening to a fuller page with the problem, the approach, the shape of the solution, and what the visitor would own. Every scenario is written in the second person and present possibility, "here is what you can build," "imagine you need to," never as a past-tense client story and never naming a real company. This reads as confident capability rather than a disclaimer, and never claims a client engagement that did not happen, protecting credibility while keeping the work specific and real-feeling. Each scenario ends with an invitation to start a conversation about a problem like it, and each is a long-tail search surface.

### 6.15 Trust

Treated as a selling surface, not a compliance afterthought, because for an intelligence the visitor hands their problem to, trust is the product. It gathers the confidence threshold, stated plainly, that the intelligence tells the visitor when a problem exceeds what it can responsibly deliver and routes to human expertise; data and the region, that the visitor's data stays in the region and is handled to local standards, a buying criterion here, not fine print; ownership, that the visitor owns what gets built, with no lock-in; security; and the trust stack, the curated and genuine signals of credibility, the technology foundations the intelligence is built on and the principles the company commits to, with no invented logos, advisors, or customers, and without centering any individual. Targets searches around AI data sovereignty in the region, who owns AI-generated solutions, and responsible AI.

### 6.16 Pricing

Removes the fear of an unknown cost without publishing a grid the company is not ready to commit to. Explains the philosophy: the visitor pays for the capability delivered and owns what is built, scoping the problem is free, and there are no per-seat licenses and no lock-in contracts. Reinforces that Stryvia does not extract rent from dependency. Chat seeded to help the visitor understand the scope of their specific problem. Targets searches around AI solution pricing and the cost of building with AI.

### 6.17 The Manifesto

This is the soul of the site and it replaces the role a founder story would have played, as a worldview rather than a biography. It states the belief plainly: that for too long the ability to build anything was gated behind assembling people, that this gate kept most ideas from ever existing, and that an intelligence you build with removes the gate and hands creation back to the person who had the idea. It names the category Stryvia is creating and the future it is building toward. It is written to be quotable and shareable, the page a believer sends to someone else. It speaks as the company, never as a person. The Manifesto is glimpsed on the homepage and lives in full here, and it is the emotional anchor that conviction-driven buyers and investors return to.

### 6.18 Why Stryvia exists

A short, institutional companion to the Manifesto that explains the company's reason for being in concrete terms: the problem it was built to solve and the conviction behind it, told as the company's purpose rather than an individual's journey. No personal narrative, no roadmap. It earns conviction without centering a person, and it gives a serious visitor and a serious investor a clear sense of why this company is here.

### 6.19 For investors (gated)

Makes the investment thesis self-evident to capital, with a short email capture before the full thesis so the company captures intent without hiding the page. It explains the market shift from AI tools to AI capability infrastructure, the defensibility with orchestration central, why a regionally anchored and data-sovereign AI company is strategically advantaged given the region's direction of travel, and the conviction and the team behind Stryvia stated institutionally. Because the rest of the site already answers most diligence questions, this page mainly connects the dots, and it reads like a sharp investment memo rather than a page that begs. It shows no product roadmap and is reached intentionally rather than through search.

### 6.20 Early access

The secondary capture, for the visitor who is convinced but not ready to start a full conversation, or who wants in before they commit. It offers a way to request access and to be among the first, which creates a sense of entry and mild exclusivity while keeping the visitor in reach. It is honest, forward, and never confesses incompleteness; it frames access as something to be granted rather than a product that is not finished. It captures the lead with whatever context the visitor offers and feeds the same destination the Chat conversion does. It appears at the close of the homepage and as a quiet, persistent option across the site for those not ready for Start.

### 6.21 Resources and learning hub

The SEO and depth engine, and over time the place where the community of users surfaces what they have built. Long-form articles and guides targeting the long-tail problem queries real buyers type, and a learning hub. Depth in a narrow vertical beats breadth across thin pages, so each article is substantial and ends in a Chat invitation framed around its specific problem. Carries the full long-tail search strategy alongside the capability, industry, problem, and example surfaces.

### 6.22 FAQ

Neutralizes objections in the visitor's own words, every answer reinforcing the positioning. Does this replace my team: no, it amplifies what you and your team can do. What if I need changes later: you own it, change it anytime. How long does it take: it depends on the problem, but it is faster than hiring and waiting. Is this just a generic AI tool: no, and here is the thinking model and the orchestration. What if my problem is too complex: the intelligence will tell you and bring in human expertise. Chat docked throughout.

### 6.23 Start

The conversion endpoint, the full Chat engagement experience, reached from the persistent Start button and from the close of every section, where the scoping conversation runs to its natural end and the "Let's build this together" handoff captures the lead with the full conversation attached.

## 7. The Chat engine

The Chat runs on Claude Opus 4.8, Anthropic's current flagship reasoning model, through the Anthropic API, with the model string `claude-opus-4-8`. Opus is chosen because the scoping interaction is the entire proof of the company and must reason about a real business problem with the depth of a senior strategist; latency is handled through streaming and a fast acknowledgment, never by downgrading the model.

Its behavior is intake and scoping. It receives the problem, asks one or two clarifying questions only when genuinely needed, detects the visitor's level of expertise from how they describe the problem, and adapts its language to match. It returns a structured scope: a restatement of the goal, an approach, the shape and rough timeline of the work, and what the visitor would own. It pushes back gently on vagueness rather than guessing, and it never overpromises.

It is designed to recognize the edge of its competence. When a problem exceeds what can be responsibly scoped, it says so and routes to a human rather than fabricating confidence, which is both the trust pillar made real and a safety property. At the natural end of a scoped response, the single "Let's build this together" action captures the full conversation and the visitor's contact details and opens the path to a human, with the conversation traveling to that human so they start informed. On interior pages, and when entered through the Solution Finder, the Chat is seeded with the gathered context, invisibly, so the first response is sharper. Conversations are handled in line with the commitments on the Trust page, with retention and residency finalized in engineering and legal and the Trust copy matching the implementation exactly. Rate limiting, input sanitization, and guardrails keep the Chat in its role and decline attempts to repurpose it.

## 8. Content and language

The voice is confident, plain, and human, the voice of a senior practitioner with nothing to prove and therefore no need to shout. Short sentences carry the weight. Jargon is avoided on every customer-facing page and used carefully only on the intelligence and investor pages where the audience expects it.

The language guide is strict because the positioning lives or dies in the words. Use: build, create, solve, customize, own, direct, your solution, the intelligence, capability, amplify, orchestrate, faster, in your control, the easiest way, without the hassle. Avoid entirely the words that pull toward a services firm, hire, our team, we deliver, done for you, and the words that pull toward a shallow tool, we teach you, you build it yourself, learn to. Never frame Stryvia as removing people from work, even though independence from dependency is the underlying truth, because that framing invites attack and converts no one. Frame everything around what the visitor gains and how effortless it is.

The SEO strategy is depth in a narrow vertical rather than breadth across shallow pages. The capability pages, the industry pages, the problem gallery, the examples, and the Resources articles target the long-tail problem queries real buyers type, where intent is high and competition is low; the homepage and overview pages reach for the broader category terms but are not expected to win those alone. Technical SEO is clean throughout: semantic structure, fast load, proper metadata, bilingual hreflang if the bilingual build proceeds, and structured data where it helps. There is one primary call to action across the whole site, Start, with Early Access as the secondary, and every other link leads back toward the conversation.

## 9. Design direction

The color palette is locked. The site is dark-first: a near-black canvas is the dominant surface, white carries the type and the open space, and a single acid-lime accent, Stryvia green `#c0fa20`, is used sparingly so it carries weight wherever it lands. The discipline is in the restraint of the green, not in muting the palette; on a dark canvas, one charged accent reads as confidence and energy at once, which is the right register for an AI intelligence. This green is Stryvia's own and is distinct from any adjacent brand.

The logo is locked. The brand system is a wide-tracked geometric STRYVIA wordmark, with a notched T carrying a small green tab, paired with an isometric interlocking-S mark rendered in green, white, and black, framed by camera focus brackets. On stryvia.ai the wordmark runs clean with no descriptor; the "Technology Driven Production" tagline from the production studio is not used here, because stryvia.ai is the intelligence platform, not the studio. If a descriptor is wanted later it is written from this positioning rather than borrowed. Usage across the site: the horizontal lockup, mark left of the wordmark, sits in the navigation; the circular black badge with the green S is the favicon, app icon, and social avatar, because the filled circle is the only version that holds at very small sizes; the wordmark alone is for the footer and watermarks. The S-mark and the focus-bracket framing recur across the site as the brand's signature device.

The layout is wide and composed, with generous dark space and a strong typographic hierarchy that does most of the design work. The feeling target is confident and futuristic, an institution with energy, never the busy noise of a generic startup landing page; restraint in how the accent is spent is the strategy. The homepage in particular is composed as a directed sequence, with calm, purposeful motion and cinematic pacing that make the page feel intentional rather than assembled, which is itself part of the proof that one intelligence stands behind it. Motion elsewhere serves pacing and atmosphere, never decorative spectacle. The Chat and the intelligence carry a consistent presence across every surface, so everything the visitor touches feels like one mind. If the bilingual build proceeds, Arabic typography is a first-class citizen with a typeface chosen for genuine quality in both scripts.

## 10. Technical architecture

The stack is a modern, fast, SEO-capable framework with server-side rendering for the content pages so they are indexable and quick, and a responsive client layer for the Chat and the interactive components. The Chat connects to the Anthropic API server-side, never exposing keys to the client. Content is structured so the capability pages, industry pages, audience pages, scenarios, and articles can be authored and expanded without engineering each addition, because the site is built to grow.

The Chat integration is a server-side proxy to the Anthropic API with streaming to the client for immediacy; conversation state is held per session and travels with the lead on conversion, and the confidence threshold and role guardrails are enforced in the system prompt and in server-side checks. The interactive components, the Solution Finder, the Speed and Cost estimator, and the "how it thinks" demonstration on the intelligence page, are built as self-contained client modules that feed their gathered context into the Chat on handoff. The conversion action, and the Early Access capture, write the captured conversation or context and contact details to a destination the team monitors and trigger the human follow-up, with the architecture treating the scoped conversation as the core asset of the lead and preserving it intact. The funnel is instrumented end to end, capturing entry points, which sections and which industries and capabilities are read, where visitors drop in the Chat or the finder, which problem types convert, and which surfaces pull search traffic, capturing problem categories rather than only page views so the data teaches the company what to build next. The site is designed mobile-first in experience, with Chat as a full-screen takeover on mobile when engaged and a single prominent button when not, full keyboard navigation and screen-reader support, first meaningful paint under two seconds on a mid-range device on a regional connection, a Chat first-response that feels immediate while reasoning streams, and regional edge delivery so the site is fast where the buyers are. If the bilingual build proceeds, it is built at the framework level with full right-to-left support, persistent language selection, and bilingual content modeling so every page exists genuinely in both languages.

The build order, for engineering only and never shown to visitors: the Chat engine is built and hardened first because the entire site stands on it, then the content framework and page templates so the capability pages, industry pages, audience pages, scenarios, and articles can be produced at volume against a consistent structure, then the interactive components. The site ships as one complete experience; the visitor never sees a partial site.
