# Stryvia.ai — Launch build brief

What is actually needed to launch something real and strong, and what to leave until later. The full detail for every item below lives in two documents you already have: the website specification and the build package. This brief is the priority layer over them. Build from the spine outward, ship, then expand on evidence.

The principle: the live Chat is the proof of the company. Anything that helps a visitor reach a real scoping conversation and convert is worth building now. Anything that does not is build-as-you-go or build-only-if-the-data-asks. Building everything up front turns a launch into a documentation project, which delays the only thing that matters, a visitor talking to the intelligence.

---

## Tier 1 — Build now. This is the launch site.

These are the only things required for a credible, converting launch. With just these, Claude Code can ship a site that proves the company and brings in leads.

**The spine, in build order.**

1. **The Chat engine.** The system prompt is in the build package, Part B, ready to lift into the build. The server route and streaming setup are in Part G. Build and harden this first; the whole site stands on it.
2. **The design system.** Tokens (color, type, spacing) and the core component states are in the build package, Part A. For launch you need the tokens (A.2 to A.5), the bracket-frame device, and these components: button (primary, secondary, ghost), text input and textarea, the Chat panel and all its states, the card, the nav, the tag, and the accordion. The rest can wait.
3. **The homepage.** Copy scene by scene is in the build package, Part C.1; layout is in Part D.2. This is the one page that must be excellent, because it carries the hero Chat.
4. **The technical foundation.** Framework, hosting, the Anthropic proxy, lead capture, analytics, and bilingual-ready scaffolding, all in the build package, Part G.

**The launch pages, beyond the homepage.** A focused set, not the full map. Each is cheap relative to its value at launch.

- How it works
- The intelligence (the Brain), for credibility with technical buyers and investors
- Trust (confidence threshold, data and region, ownership, security)
- Pricing (the short philosophy version)
- The Manifesto (the soul of the site, and cheap to build)
- FAQ (real answers are already written in the build package, Part C.3)
- Start, with Early access as the secondary capture
- For investors, gated (one page, and you have stated investor interest as a goal, so it earns its place at launch)
- A small set of capability pages, five to six, the ones your earliest visitors are most likely to search for, built from the capability template in the build package, Part C.4. Suggested launch five: build a product or app; launch a new venture; creative and content production; marketing and growth; operations and automation. Add the rest later from the same template.
- A handful of scenarios, written in present possibility from the scenario template, surfaced on the homepage and the relevant capability pages. These are your proof in place of case studies.

That is the entire launch. It is real, it converts, and nothing in it is faked or half-built.

---

## Tier 2 — Build during, or shortly after launch.

Valuable, but not required to prove the company. Scaffold from templates as capacity allows; refine in place rather than perfecting up front.

- The remaining capability pages, growing toward the full set as the thinking-model documentation defines the domains
- The industry layer, the sector pages, added as you see which industries actually arrive
- The full filterable galleries as standalone pages: Problem gallery, Outcome showcase, Examples
- The standalone persuasion pages whose ideas the homepage already touches: Speed and cost, Works with everything, Compare, See it in control
- The audience pages, the full "for you" doors; the homepage already provides the entry, the dedicated pages deepen it
- Bilingual Arabic content population, if you confirm it; the build is engineered ready for it from day one
- Resources, the first articles, beginning the long-tail search compounding

---

## Tier 3 — Build only if the data asks.

Good ideas, expensive to build well, and unproven as necessary. The live Chat already does the proving, so do not build these on faith.

- The Solution Finder
- The Speed and Cost estimator
- The intelligence explorer
- The learning hub and community surfacing

The trigger to build any of them: analytics showing visitors getting lost before they reach the Chat, bouncing without engaging, or a specific page pulling enough demand that it needs its own tool. Until the data says so, the effort is better spent making the Chat brilliant.

---

## The start-here sequence

One repository. Build the design-system component library first, then the Chat engine and its route, then the homepage, then the remaining Tier 1 pages from the templates, then content and the bilingual scaffolding. Ship. Watch the funnel. Add Tier 2 as content and capacity allow. Add Tier 3 only when the data asks.

---

## Still open, and gating nothing in Tier 1

Two inputs raise the ceiling on Tier 2 depth, and neither blocks the launch.

The thinking-model documentation that defines your domains, which turns the capability and industry sets from a strong launch handful into the full specific map.

The yes or no on populating Arabic at launch, the only product decision still open. The build is engineered bilingual-ready regardless; the question is only whether the Arabic ships day one or shortly after.
